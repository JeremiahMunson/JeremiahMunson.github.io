Computer Science and Mathematics Minors

I love the experiences I've gotten at Carthage College. Carthage is currently the lead institute for the Wisconsin Space Grant Consortium (WSGC). My freshman year of college I was fortunate enough to help start the Canopy Near-Infrared Observing Project (CaNOP) CubeSat, the WSGC's first CubeSat mission. I have been the Systems Engineer for the CaNOP CubeSat since June 2016. I was also able to participate in a conceptual design competition for a future air vehicle. I have had the pleasure of also being involved in the Computer Science department at Carthage College. I joined the Computer Science Club and participated in four hackathons with other Carthage CS students. My friend Joe Wonsil and I won the Security and Privacy category at the Madhacks-Mini for our project PeekAWho. This was a 12-hour hackathon hosted by the University of Wisconsin - Madison in spring 2018. I also joined the Gamma Gamma Chapter of Phi Kappa Sigma International Fraternity. Joining the fraternity greatly increase my confidence and made me more outgoing, an amazing feat considering how shy I was before. I have noticed a significant improvement in my networking capabilities and confidence presenting at different conferences. Through my experiences and the connections I have made at Carthage College I was selected as a Research Associate for the NASA Space Academy at NASA Glenn Research Center for the summer of 2018.

I have a 3.84 overall GPA on a 4.00 scale with a 3.89 GPA in my major, a 3.89 GPA in my Mathematics minor, and a 4.00 GPA in my Computer Science minor.
