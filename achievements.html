Fall 2015, Spring 2016, Fall 2016, Spring 2017, Fall 2017, Spring 2018

I may not be able to make summa cum laude (U.S. undergraduate: with highest honors) with my grades, but I'd rather get magna cum laude (with great honor) and place at hackathons and build a satellite (although doing all that AND getting summa cum laude is ideal).

Security and Privacy Winner: MadHacks-Mini, University of Wisconsin-Madison

April 2018

My friend Joe Wonsil and I won the Security and Privacy category at the MadHacks Mini Hackathon in April 2018. Over the entire 12 hour hackathon we created from scratch a program in Python that used a facial recognition library (face_recognition) and sockets to communicate between computers. The demoed project is available on GitHub titled PeekAWho. For additional information you can visit our devpost submission here.

Outstanding Physics Student Award: Department of Physics and Astronomy, Carthage College

Eagle Scout

November 2015

To achieve the rank of Eagle Scout one must be highly motivated and capable of dedicating themselves to a group for years. There are many specific requirements to achieve the rank but most notably it is required the scout create, plan, lead, and execute a service project bettering his church, school, or community.

My project was building and placing 5 benches and 17 trail signs along the trails scaling a bluff leading to an overlook with a spectacular view of my hometown. Before the project was done I was being thanked by people I had never met before who loved the trails and used the benches and signs. The project has allowed people who would otherwise not be able to make the steep climb up the bluff enjoy the trails as I did and still do.
